Barcelona

About

I visited Barcelona for a weekend over the summer of 2023. It was part of my United Airlines internship benefits. The benefits allowed me to be on standby on any airplane that had room. That meant that I was able to fly all over from any destination that United Airlines flies to. I flew to Barcelona by myself to do a solo trip. I stayed at a hostel with 8 other girls in the room and joined a Gaudí tour to see his most famous attractions

Highlights of the Trip

  • I loved visiting the La Sagrada Familia again and seeing how it has changed since I last saw it in 2016. While the architecture is still not finished, the church always amazes me.
  • I finally got to visit all the different Gaudí attractions including Park Güell and Casa Batillò, and explore them with a guided tour.
  • Barcelona is famous for their tapas, and I got to try some at Cervercia Catalana, a very famous tapas restaurant. I got to skip the super long line!
  • It was super exciting to see the night life on the busy street of Las Ramblas. There were street vendors on the road that sold food and desserts as well as lively groups of people hanging out late at night.

La Sagrada Familia

La Sagrada Familia

The La Sagrada Familia’s is Gaudí’s unfinished masterpiece and is still being built today. Gaudí started this church to use architecture to showcase Christ’s lifetime. Each part of La Sagrada Familia is constructed with a purpose to tell a story about Christ. I would recommend having an audio-guided tour that details all the history and significance of La Sagrada Familia as you walk through the church.

Park Güell

Park Güell

Park Güell was made to be a elite community for the rich in Barcelona, but it failed due to lack of residents buying the estates. The Park itself was made to complement the nature it was built on, with trees and wildlife incorporated into the designs. It has a gorgeous view of Barcelona’s horizon.

Casa Batlló

Casa Batlló

Casa Batlló, also known as the House of Dragon due to the scale-like roof was a residential house made by Gaudí for Joseph Battló, a wealthy aristocrat. It is famous for the utlization of windows to allow natural light in and the bright, vivid colors that fill up the exterior and interior of the house.
